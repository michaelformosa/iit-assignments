Kenneth Thompson

  • Designed & Implemented Unix Operating System
  • Computer Designer

1. What kind of work is done by the IT professional?

As a Computer designer, Kenneth's main tasks were to primarily code and create new programming languages which would result in a new operating system such as unix. He has worked at Google previously when he co-invented the Go programming language. Kenneth establishes source codes into the compiler which generates machine language.

2. What kinds of people does the IT professional interact with? Are there other IT professionals? Clients? Investors? The general public?

Kenneth interacts with many of his colleagues when he created unix. The teamwork and effort required was essential in ensuring the success of the programming language and operating system. He wouldn’t have had interaction with users, clients or the general public when creating unix but once the operating system was in the testing stage, he would perform a usability test on clients for feedback on improvements for general use of the operating system.

3. Where does the IT professional spend most of their time?

During the development stage of unix, Kenneth would have spent most of his time in his computer lab with his colleagues for excellent communication. During the testing stage of unix, he may have gone to a public place or a friends house to conduct his usability testing.

4. What aspect of their position is most challenging?

The most challenging aspect of Kenneth's position as a computer designer has been designing the programming language GO, he spent most of his time developing it with his colleagues which, despite the difficult times with errors and frustration, they managed to successfully complete the project. Later on, Kenneth began developing the unix operating system by which the most challenging aspect was the programming component of the operating system.

Dan Riccio

Dan leads the Mac, iPhone, iPad and iPod engineering teams, being a key contributor to hardware components of each product. Dan’s main role as a hardware engineer includes designing and testing computer hardware components, analyzing test data, eliminating errors, modifying existing hardware, and designing hardware for other electrical devices such as the iphones.

2. What kinds of people does the IT professional interact with? Are there other IT professionals? Clients? Investors? The general public?

Dan interacts with many other IT professionals as he leads the hardware engineer team, he ensures they are up to date with the tasks and perform them efficiently. Dan may interact with the apple department team by testing the functionality of the hardware to ensure it runs as intended

3. Where does the IT professional spend most of their time?

Dan spends the majority of his time at Apple Headquarters when working on his hardware development tasks. This is also the place when he must attend meetings with the CEO and other department managers to discuss their progress

4. What aspect of their position is most challenging?

The most challenging part of Dan’s duties are the budget of the project, the project schedule, staying current with the latest technologies and trends, the bigger volume of projects, and finally, the complex legal matters involving patents and IP protection.
